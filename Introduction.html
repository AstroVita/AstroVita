Naming Of The Project

The name AstroVita is a combination of Astro (Astronomy) which is the study of the great Universe(space) and Vita (bio) which means life. The combined word AstroVita means life in space.

When our team tried to find a good name for this project, my friend Shaurya said we should also use some other languages. Then we saw Vita, which means life in Latin. If we could make a good word with Vita, we could name our project after that.

Since this project was being built in space we named it AstroVita. After getting permission from our mentor, it was official.

AstroVita Symbolises Our Project’s Main aim of creating a habitat for the human race to survive happily.

General Presentation Of the Base

Overview:- The Space Station AstroVita will be a continuing toroidal space settlement located at L1 {1,500,000 km (932,000 mi) from Earth}. It will be able to sustain 2000 passengers (including Farmers, Scientists, Teachers, Industry Workers, etc.)

The structure consists of 4 major components

  • Central Hub (Cylinder)

    • It is the core of the settlement holding all the parts together by spokes.
    • It also contains an elevator that will work using an EM(ElectroMagnet).
    • It will have no gravity.
    • On the top surface of the Central Hub, there will be a Docking Station. Whereas on the bottom surface will be the Thrusters & Boosters

  • Toroids Or Torii

    • These toroids are going to contain the main compartments of our structure (such as the residential Area, Agriculture Area, Industrial Area, Recreation Zone, etc)
    • There will be 4 toroids (2 Large & 2 Small)

  • Spokes

    • They will be the connectors of the Toroid/Solar panels to the Central Hub.
    • It will follow the diameter of the Toroids.
    • Allow Easy access to the other side of the Toroid or into the Hub.

  • Solar Panels

    • The Solar Panels will allow us to run the AstroVita by making renewable energy from the Sun’s rays.

Key Features:-

  • Self-Sufficiency

    • By Using
      • Solar Panels for Energy
      • Hydroponics and Aeroponics for Agriculture
      • Photosynthesis for Oxygen
      • Recycling Waste Water to be used in Agriculture
      • Algaculture for Fuel and Fertilizer
      • Thermal Rejection for Heat Regulation

  • Habitability

    • Toroid
      • Designed for Human Habitation (Industries, Housing Communities, Agriculture, etc.)
      • Artificial Gravity will be generated using Centrifugal Force
    • Conditions
      • LED Lights and Hidden Speakers make an artificial sky that can stipulate weather
      • Combined with mist releasers, water sprinklers can precipitate(eg: Rain & Snow)

Justification For The Shape

S.No. Shape Advantages Disadvantages
1 Cylinder Cylinders provide a structurally efficient design that can distribute stress evenly,making them resilient under rotational forces and external pressures in space.

A cylinder offers a significant amount of interior space for habitats, central control tower, especially if the cylinder is large and hollow.

Cylinders can be designed in modular sections, making them easier to assemble and expand over time.

Cylinders can be shielded with regolith or water, protecting inhabitants from harmful cosmic rays and solar radiation 		Building a large, pressurised, and rotating cylindrical habitat in Space presents significant engineering and logistical challenges.

Rotating the cylinder to generate artificial gravity requires energy, especially for maintaining precise rotation speeds and stabilising gyroscopic effects.

Large cylinders are susceptible to micrometeoroid impacts and require robust materials to prevent catastrophic failure in the vacuum of space.

While radiation can be mitigated, achieving sufficient shielding while keeping the cylinder light enough for construction and launch can be difficult.
